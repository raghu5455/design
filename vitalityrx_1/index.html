    Estradiol

    A type of estrogen, is vital for bone health, brain function, and male fertility. It aids in testicular fluid balance. Elevated levels, often from testosterone conversion by aromatase in belly fat, can lead to weight gain and increased fat around the chest and belly. Monitoring estradiol is crucial for overall health.

    Sex Hormone Binding Globulin (SHBG)

    Produced by the liver, regulates hormone availability in your body. Imbalances, whether high or low, suggest an excess or deficiency of hormones for use. These levels can also indicate liver issues, diabetes, or abnormal thyroid function.

    Luteinizing Hormone

    A key factor in men’s health. It stimulates testosterone production in the testicles, crucial for fertility and overall well-being. Low LH levels may signal issues with the pituitary or hypothalamus, leading to low testosterone and potential fertility concerns.

    Free & Total Testosterone

    Mainly from the testes and a bit from the adrenal glands, isn’t just a male sex hormone. It affects bone density, libido, fat distribution, and muscle. Most testosterone is bound to proteins like albumin and SHBG, but the active form — free testosterone — is unattached

    Prostate-Specific Antigen

     A protein from the prostate, produced by both cancerous and noncancerous tissue. The test measures PSA levels in the blood to screen for prostate health and cancer. Elevated levels may signal an enlarged prostate or prostate cancer.
